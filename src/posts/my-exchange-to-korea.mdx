I lived and studied at Korea University in Seoul for 5 months of my bachelor’s. The entire experience was surreal and it feels like I left a different life there that I may never have again. My lifestyle was completely different there, but the biggest difference was that I ate out every single day for every single meal.

Went on a weekend trip to Japan during the semester, and passed through japan for two weeks after the semester. The two countries were quite different in food and other cultural preservation strategies. Japan seemed to be holding on to old customs, while Korea tried to advance as fast as possible.

To be completely stark it wasn’t all sunshine and rainbows; it did come with a lot of stress-factors. Firstly a bunch of bureaucratic stuff to get admission to Korea University, visa, and an Alien Registration Card (national ID). There was also timezones and distance isolating be from my Norwegian friend group. Seoul is a city that absolutely never sleeps, so there was also constant stimuli (for better or worse).

I will definitely come back and visit, but I can’t imagine myself living and working there again in my adult years, unless their working conditions get drastically better.

I took 4 courses at Korea University: 

- Contemporary Korea
    - Covered the demography of Korea. Lectures about Korean society and history in relation to western countries and Japan.
- Popular Culture and Society
    - A course where we discussed and wrote about the reflection of society in popular culture. The professor used The Greatest Showman as the primary example throughout the course and had us reflect on different aspects of society and how they depicted it in the movie
- Information Security
    - A heck ton of encryption algorithms and ciphers.
- System Programming
    - Low-level programming in Rust. Focusing on the heap and central rust concepts like the ownership/borrowing concept as well as the lifetimes
